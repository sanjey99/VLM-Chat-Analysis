## Design Context

### Users
Nika employees exploring hangout spots around Singapore. Used casually — lunchtime discovery, after-work planning, team dinner research. Not under time pressure. The interface is a companion for exploration, not a productivity tool.

### Brand Personality
Warm, knowledgeable, local — like a friend who's lived in Singapore for years and always knows a good spot. Conversational but never chatty. Confident recommendations without being prescriptive. The emotional goal: delight and a sense of discovery, like finding a place you didn't know existed.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark — deep warm charcoal (not blue-black, not pure black), tinted toward amber
- **Accent**: Warm amber / terracotta — evokes paper maps, café tables, worn signage. NOT cyan, NOT purple, NOT neon.
- **Typography**: Something with warmth and editorial character. Bricolage Grotesque for UI elements + Source Serif 4 for chat messages (friend writing you a note).
- **Feel**: A well-worn travel journal meets a cozy map room. Cartographic, tactile, human.
- **Anti-references**: Generic AI chatbot (white chat bubbles on gray), corporate dashboard (KPI cards, enterprise blue), sci-fi/cyberpunk (neon grids, glowing cyan).

### Design Principles
1. **The map is the hero** — the chat panel assists, never dominates. 70% map, 30% sidebar.
2. **Warmth over precision** — rounded but not bubbly, soft shadows not hard borders, amber not blue.
3. **Friend, not bot** — chat messages should feel handwritten, not API-generated. Typography and spacing matter here.
4. **Discovery, not search** — animate results arriving on the map. Make finding a place feel like an event.
5. **No chrome, no clutter** — every UI element must earn its place. If it doesn't serve the user's query or the map, remove it.
